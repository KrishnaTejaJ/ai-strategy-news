## 1. The National Security Case for Limiting China’s Access to Advanced U.S. Compute: Evidence from PLA Procurement Documents
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [The National Security Case for Limiting China’s Access to Advanced U.S. Compute: Evidence from PLA Procurement Documents](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/the-national-security-case-for-limiting-chinas-access-to-advanced-u-s-compute-evidence-from-pla-procurement-documents/)  

**So What:** This matters because it highlights the risks of China's access to advanced US computing capabilities for its military modernization

## 2. Airwaves of Power: Why the Pentagon Should Shift to a Commercial-First Spectrum Model
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [Airwaves of Power: Why the Pentagon Should Shift to a Commercial-First Spectrum Model](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/airwaves-of-power-why-the-pentagon-should-shift-to-a-commercial-first-spectrum-model/)  

**So What:** This matters because a commercial-first spectrum model could generate significant economic benefits while enhancing national security

The current allocation of electromagnetic spectrum reflects outdated policy choices, prioritizing national security uses over commercial applications. Shifting to a commercial-first model could unlock significant economic value, foster innovation, and improve the US position in the global tech competition. This approach requires careful consideration of the trade-offs between national security and economic interests.

**Watch For:** Pentagon initiatives to reform its electromagnetic spectrum policy and allocate more spectrum for commercial use
