## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's push for AI independence undermines US leverage in the tech competition between the two nations

China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, including developing alternatives to American chips, will reduce its dependence on US technology and decrease the effectiveness of US export controls. This move has significant implications for the US-China tech competition, as it may limit the US's ability to restrict China's access to advanced technologies. The development of domestic AI capabilities will also have second-order effects on China's overall technological ecosystem

**Watch For:** Monitor the progress of Chinese companies like DeepSeek and Huawei in developing alternative chips and assess the potential impact on US export control policies

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch evaluation of AI models by the US government marks a significant step towards AI governance and regulation

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate unreleased AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks before launch demonstrates a growing recognition of the need for AI governance. This move has the potential to set a precedent for the regulation of AI development and deployment in the US. The involvement of major tech companies also highlights the increasing importance of public-private partnerships in addressing AI-related risks

**Watch For:** Track the implementation and outcomes of these agreements, and assess their potential impact on the development of AI governance frameworks in the US

## 4. The Chain of Peace: Do Supply Chain Chokepoints Deter War?
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The Chain of Peace: Do Supply Chain Chokepoints Deter War?](https://warontherocks.com/the-chain-of-peace-do-supply-chain-chokepoints-deter-war/)  

**So What:** The concentration of critical semiconductor manufacturing capabilities in a few key locations creates potential chokepoints that could deter conflict

The fact that companies like ASML and TSMC dominate the production of advanced semiconductors creates a critical dependency that could serve as a deterrent to conflict. The potential disruption of these supply chains poses significant risks to the global economy, and this could be a major consideration for nations contemplating military action. The US and its allies may be able to leverage these supply chain chokepoints to enhance their diplomatic and economic leverage

**Watch For:** Assess the US and its allies' ability to utilize supply chain dependencies as a tool of economic statecraft and monitor the development of policies aimed at mitigating the risks associated with critical supply chain chokepoints

## 5. Google DeepMind is worried about what happens when millions of agents start to interact
**Source:** Artificial intelligence – MIT Technology Review  
**Link:** [Google DeepMind is worried about what happens when millions of agents start to interact](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/11/1138794/google-deepmind-is-worried-about-what-happens-when-millions-of-agents-start-to-interact/)  

**So What:** The potential risks associated with the interaction of multiple AI agents highlight the need for more research on AI governance and safety

Google DeepMind's concerns about the potential dangers of situations where millions of AI agents interact with each other online underscore the need for more research on AI safety and governance. As AI systems become increasingly ubiquitous, the potential risks associated with their interaction will grow, and it is essential to develop frameworks for mitigating these risks. The development of AI governance frameworks will need to take into account the potential risks associated with the interaction of multiple AI agents

**Watch For:** Track the progress of research on AI safety and governance, particularly with regards to the interaction of multiple AI agents, and assess the potential implications for the development of AI-related policies
