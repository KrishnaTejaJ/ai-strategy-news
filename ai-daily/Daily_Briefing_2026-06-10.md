## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's pursuit of AI independence undermines US leverage in the tech competition.

China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, particularly through companies like DeepSeek and Huawei, signal a deliberate attempt to reduce dependence on American chips. This move not only accelerates China's technological self-sufficiency but also challenges the efficacy of US export controls as a tool for technological and geopolitical leverage. The development of domestic AI capabilities will likely bolster China's position in the global tech landscape.

**Watch For:** Further advancements in China's domestic chip production and AI model development to gauge the pace of its independence.

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch AI model evaluations by the US government mark a significant step in AI governance.

The agreements between major tech companies and the US government to evaluate AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks before launch indicate a growing recognition of the need for proactive AI governance. This collaborative approach could set a precedent for how governments engage with the development of advanced technologies, potentially influencing global standards for AI safety and security. It reflects an evolving landscape where technological advancement is increasingly intertwined with national security considerations.

**Watch For:** The implementation details and outcomes of these evaluations to understand their impact on AI development and governance.

## 3. The Fault Lines in China’s Power
**Source:** FA RSS  
**Link:** [The Fault Lines in China’s Power](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/china/fault-lines-chinas-power-ratner-danby)  

**So What:** Understanding China's vulnerabilities is crucial for the US to build effective leverage.

Recognizing the fault lines in China's power structure and economy is essential for the US to develop a strategic approach to competition. This involves identifying areas where China is most vulnerable to pressure, such as its reliance on foreign technology, and using these as points of leverage. The US must balance its competitive stance with a nuanced understanding of China's internal dynamics to avoid miscalculations that could escalate tensions.

**Watch For:** Shifts in China's domestic policies and external relations that could indicate either an increase in vulnerabilities or a strengthening of its position.
