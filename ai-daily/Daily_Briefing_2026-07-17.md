## 2. How did the government decide OpenAI’s frontier model was safe to release?
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [How did the government decide OpenAI’s frontier model was safe to release?](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/how-did-the-government-decide-openais-frontier-model-was-safe-to-release/)  

**So What:** Lack of transparency in US government evaluation and approval of advanced AI models raises concerns about AI governance and safety

The opaque process for evaluating and approving AI models for public release undermines trust in AI governance and highlights the need for more transparent and accountable decision-making processes. This lack of transparency also has implications for the development of AI safety standards and the ability to mitigate potential risks associated with advanced AI models. Moreover, it may create an uneven playing field for AI developers, with some companies potentially receiving more favorable treatment than others

**Watch For:** Developments in AI governance frameworks and regulations that address transparency and accountability in AI model evaluation and approval processes

## 4. The US is advancing AI safety through state and federal action
**Source:** OpenAI News  
**Link:** [The US is advancing AI safety through state and federal action](https://openai.com/index/advancing-ai-safety-through-state-and-federal-action)  

**So What:** A 'reverse federalism' approach to AI governance, where state laws inform national frameworks, may be an effective way to address AI safety and regulatory challenges

This approach allows for experimentation and innovation at the state level, which can inform and shape federal policies and regulations. By leveraging state-level initiatives, the US can develop more effective and adaptive AI governance frameworks that balance safety, innovation, and democratic values. However, it also raises questions about the potential for inconsistent or conflicting regulations across states and the need for coordination and standardization

**Watch For:** State-level AI regulations and initiatives that may influence federal policy and the development of national AI governance frameworks
