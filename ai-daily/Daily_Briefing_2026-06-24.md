## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's efforts to build a domestic A.I. ecosystem undermine US leverage in the tech competition and could reduce reliance on US components.

China's push for A.I. independence is a direct response to US export controls, underscoring the intensifying tech competition between the two nations. This development could have significant second-order effects, including reduced US influence over China's tech sector and increased competition in the global A.I. market. As China develops its own A.I. capabilities, it may also reduce its reliance on US semiconductor components, potentially disrupting global supply chains.

**Watch For:** Monitoring the progress of Chinese companies like DeepSeek and Huawei in developing alternatives to American chips will be crucial in assessing the impact of US export controls on China's A.I. ambitions.

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology

The willingness of Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate their unreleased AI models underscores the growing recognition of the need for AI governance and regulation. **Watch For:** The outcomes of these evaluations and the subsequent regulatory responses will be important to monitor, as they may shape the future of AI development and deployment in the US.
