## 3. Backlash against data centers could cost the US its AI edge
**Source:** Atlantic Council  
**Link:** [Backlash against data centers could cost the US its AI edge](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/energysource/backlash-against-data-centers-could-cost-the-us-its-ai-edge/)  

**So What:** This matters because data centers are crucial for AI development and deployment, and a backlash could hinder US competitiveness in the global AI race

The US must balance these concerns with the need to maintain its AI leadership, potentially through more sustainable data center designs or innovative solutions

**Watch For:** Policy responses to data center backlash, such as regulations or incentives for sustainable data centers, and their impact on US AI development and competitiveness

## 4. The US is advancing AI safety through state and federal action

## 5. Before Q-Day: The Race to Quantum First
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [Before Q-Day: The Race to Quantum First](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/before-q-day-the-race-to-quantum-first/)  

**So What:** This matters because the advent of quantum computing poses significant risks to current encryption methods, which underpin global digital security, and could give an advantage to the first country to develop practical quantum capabilities

The rapid progress in quantum computing, as evidenced by the lowered estimates for breaking encryption, poses an existential threat to current digital security systems. The development of quantum-resistant encryption methods is crucial, and the 'race to quantum first' may drive innovation in this area. The US and China are likely to be at the forefront of this development, with significant implications for their respective national security and technological competitiveness

**Watch For:** Breakthroughs in quantum-resistant encryption methods and the development of practical quantum computing capabilities, particularly in the context of the US-China tech competition and global digital security
