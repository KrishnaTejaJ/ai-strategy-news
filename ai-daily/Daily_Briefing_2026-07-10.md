## 1. Trump administration’s AI crackdown opens door for China to close gap
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Trump administration’s AI crackdown opens door for China to close gap](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/trump-administrations-ai-crackdown-opens-door-for-china-to-close-gap/)  

**So What:** US AI restrictions may inadvertently accelerate China's AI progress, narrowing the gap between the two nations.

The US restrictions on AI may have unintended consequences, such as driving Chinese innovation and investment in the sector, potentially eroding the US lead in AI. This could have significant implications for the US-China tech competition, as China may be able to leverage its growing AI capabilities to gain a strategic advantage. The impact of these restrictions on the global semiconductor supply chain also warrants attention.

**Watch For:** China's AI advancements and potential collaborations with other nations to counterbalance US restrictions.

## 2. The Pentagon’s AI Strategy Has a Funding Problem
**Source:** War on the Rocks

The Pentagon's AI strategy, though ambitious, faces significant funding challenges that could limit its execution and impact.
