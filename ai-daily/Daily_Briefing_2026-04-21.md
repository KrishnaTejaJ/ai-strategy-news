## 4. The United States Is Repeating Its Silicon Mistake with Gallium Nitride
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The United States Is Repeating Its Silicon Mistake with Gallium Nitride](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-united-states-is-repeating-its-silicon-mistake-with-gallium-nitride/)  

**So What:** The US is vulnerable to supply chain disruptions due to its lack of domestic gallium reserves, mirroring past mistakes with silicon

The article highlights the alarming similarity between the US's current situation with gallium nitride and its past experience with silicon. The US pioneered silicon semiconductor technology but lost its domestic manufacturing capabilities, leading to dependence on international suppliers. The current situation with gallium nitride, a critical mineral for future microchips, poses significant risks to US national security and economic interests. China's export controls and ban on gallium exports to the US exacerbate this vulnerability

**Watch For:** US efforts to develop domestic gallium reserves, diversify supply chains, and reduce dependence on Chinese exports
