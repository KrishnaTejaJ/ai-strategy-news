## 1. Trump administration’s AI crackdown opens door for China to close gap
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Trump administration’s AI crackdown opens door for China to close gap](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/trump-administrations-ai-crackdown-opens-door-for-china-to-close-gap/)  

**So What:** US AI restrictions may inadvertently accelerate China's AI development, narrowing the US-China AI gap.

The US restrictions on AI may lead to a brain drain of top AI talent to China, while also driving Chinese investment in domestic AI research, potentially eroding US dominance in the field. This could have significant implications for the future of US-China tech competition. Furthermore, China's rapid progress in AI may enable it to leapfrog the US in certain areas, such as natural language processing or computer vision.

**Watch For:** Monitor Chinese AI research publications and patent filings to track the pace of China's AI development.

## 2. The Chemistry of Chips
**Source:** ChinaTalk  
**Link:** [The Chemistry of Chips](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/the-chemistry-of-chips)  

**So What:** Understanding the chemistry of chipmaking is crucial for navigating the complexities of semi-conductor supply chains.

## 3. A Better Way to Build AI
**Source:** FA RSS  
**Link:** [A Better Way to Build AI](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/united-states/better-way-build-ai)  

**So What:** Effective AI governance requires engaging local communities and addressing their concerns about AI development.

## 4. AI is the next major biodefense tech priority
**Source:** Atlantic Council  
**Link:** [AI is the next major biodefense tech priority](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/in-depth-research-reports/issue-brief/ai-is-the-next-major-biodefense-tech-priority/)  

**So What:** The intersection of AI and biotechnology poses new challenges and opportunities for biodefense, with significant implications for AI governance and US-China tech competition.

The article highlights the potential for AI to be used in the creation and detection of biological threats, which raises concerns about the misuse of AI in this context. This could lead to a new era of biodefense competition between the US and China, with each side seeking to develop and deploy AI-powered biodefense capabilities. Effective governance of AI in biodefense will be critical to preventing accidents and misuse.

**Watch For:** Follow developments in US and Chinese biodefense research, particularly with regards to AI-powered detection and response systems.

## 5. Not Just Rare Earths – Is This China’s Next Economic Weapon?
**Source:** The Diplomat  
**Link:** [Not Just Rare Earths – Is This China’s Next Economic Weapon?](https://thediplomat.com/2026/07/not-just-rare-earths-is-this-chinas-next-economic-weapon/)  

**So What:** China's dominance in the fluorine value chain could provide a new source of economic leverage, with implications for US-China trade relations and semi-conductor supply chains.

The article draws attention to China's significant control over the fluorine value chain, which is a critical input for various industries, including semi-conductors and pharmaceuticals. This could enable China to exert pressure on its trading partners, including the US, by restricting access to fluorine-based products or using its dominance as a bargaining chip in trade negotiations. Furthermore, it may also create new dependencies in global supply chains, particularly in the semi-conductor industry.

**Watch For:** Monitor Chinese trade policies and announcements related to fluorine-based products, as well as US efforts to diversify its supply chain and reduce dependence on Chinese fluorine exports.
