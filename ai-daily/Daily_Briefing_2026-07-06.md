## 1. Trump administration’s AI crackdown opens door for China to close gap
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Trump administration’s AI crackdown opens door for China to close gap](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/trump-administrations-ai-crackdown-opens-door-for-china-to-close-gap/)  

**So What:** US AI restrictions may inadvertently accelerate China's AI advancements, narrowing the gap with the US

The Trump administration's AI crackdown could have unintended second-order effects, such as driving Chinese innovation and investment in AI, potentially eroding the US's competitive edge. This highlights the need for a nuanced approach to AI governance, balancing national security concerns with the risk of accelerating rival nations' progress. The dynamic interplay between US policy and Chinese technological advancements will be crucial to monitor

**Watch For:** China's progress in developing indigenous AI capabilities and potential responses from the US to adapt its AI governance strategy

## 2. U.S. Semiconductors and China’s AI Military Ambitions
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [U.S. Semiconductors and China’s AI Military Ambitions](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/u-s-semiconductors-and-chinas-ai-military-ambitions/)  

**So What:** Relaxing US restrictions on AI semiconductor exports to China could compromise long-term US technological advantage and national security

The issue of semiconductor exports to China is a critical juncture in the US-China tech competition, with significant implications for AI governance and military applications. Allowing China to access advanced semiconductors could enhance its military capabilities, while restricting exports may slow China's AI progress but also risk pushing it to develop indigenous alternatives. This dilemma underscores the complexities of balancing economic interests with national security concerns

**Watch For:** US policy decisions on semiconductor export controls and China's efforts to develop domestic semiconductor production capabilities

## 5. Understanding China’s Party-State Intelligence System
**Source:** The Diplomat  
**Link:** [Understanding China’s Party-State Intelligence System](https://thediplomat.com/2026/07/understanding-chinas-party-state-intelligence-system/)
