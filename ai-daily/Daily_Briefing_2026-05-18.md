## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's efforts to build a domestic A.I. ecosystem weaken US leverage over China's tech sector

China's acceleration of domestic A.I. development, particularly in alternatives to American chips, undermines the effectiveness of US export controls and highlights the need for a more nuanced US strategy to address China's growing tech capabilities. This trend is likely to exacerbate US-China tech competition, with significant implications for global semiconductor supply chains. As China reduces its dependence on US tech, the US may need to reassess its approach to AI governance and cooperation.

**Watch For:** China's progress in developing domestic A.I. chips and potential US responses to mitigate the loss of leverage

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/beyond-ai-what-the-pentagon-is-missing-with-its-trimmed-critical-technologies-list/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's narrow focus on critical technologies may overlook key capability gaps in emerging tech

The Pentagon's decision to trim its list of critical technologies, while a positive step towards prioritization, may inadvertently overlook crucial emerging technologies that could address existing capability gaps. This narrowing of focus could have significant second-order effects, including reduced investment in AI governance and semi-conductor research, which are critical to maintaining US technological superiority. A more nuanced approach to prioritizing and funding emerging technologies is necessary to ensure long-term military innovation.

**Watch For:** The Pentagon's future investments in emerging technologies and potential revisions to its critical technologies list

## 3. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch testing of AI models by the US government could enhance AI governance and cybersecurity

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate unreleased AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks before launch represent a significant step towards more effective AI governance. This collaborative approach could set a precedent for future public-private partnerships in AI development, potentially mitigating the risks associated with AI adoption. However, it also raises questions about the balance between innovation and regulation in the US AI sector.

**Watch For:** The impact of pre-launch testing on the development and deployment of AI models, as well as potential expansions of this collaborative approach

## 4. A U.S. Economic Security “Latency Fund”
**Source:** ChinaTalk  
**Link:** [A U.S. Economic Security “Latency Fund”](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/a-us-economic-security-latency-fund)  

**So What:** A US 'Latency Fund' could provide a strategic economic security advantage in the face of growing US-China competition

The concept of a US 'Latency Fund' for economic security, inspired by the idea of 'nuclear latency', suggests a proactive approach to maintaining a strategic economic advantage. Such a fund could enable the US to rapidly respond to emerging economic security threats, including disruptions to semi-conductor supply chains. By investing in latent economic capabilities, the US could reduce its vulnerability to external economic pressures and enhance its ability to shape the global economic landscape.

**Watch For:** Potential developments in US economic security policy, including the establishment of a 'Latency Fund' or similar initiatives

The article's discussion of 'respect, leverage, and the new realism' in US-China relations highlights the need for a more nuanced understanding of the complex dynamics at play.
