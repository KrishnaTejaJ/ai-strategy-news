## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's acceleration of domestic AI ecosystem development undermines US leverage through export controls.

China's efforts to build alternatives to American chips, driven by companies like DeepSeek and Huawei, signal a significant shift towards reducing dependence on US technology. This move not only weakens the US's ability to exert pressure through export controls but also marks a crucial step in China's pursuit of AI independence, potentially altering the global AI landscape. The implications of this development extend to the broader US-China tech competition, where self-sufficiency in critical technologies becomes a strategic advantage.

**Watch For:** Monitoring the progress of Chinese companies in developing competitive AI chip technologies and their impact on the global semiconductor supply chain.

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch government evaluation of AI models by major tech companies enhances national security and could set a precedent for AI governance.

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, xAI, and the US government to evaluate AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks before launch represent a significant collaboration aimed at mitigating potential threats. This proactive approach not only reflects the evolving landscape of AI governance but also underscores the tech industry's recognition of its role in ensuring national security. The success of these evaluations could pave the way for standardized practices in AI model assessments, influencing the development of AI governance frameworks globally.

**Watch For:** The outcomes of these evaluations and how they shape future regulatory approaches to AI, particularly in terms of setting industry-wide standards for AI model security and transparency.

## 4. The Chain of Peace: Do Supply Chain Chokepoints Deter War?
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The Chain of Peace: Do Supply Chain Chokepoints Deter War?](https://warontherocks.com/the-chain-of-peace-do-supply-chain-chokepoints-deter-war/)  

**So What:** The dependence on specific technologies, like ASML's lithography systems, can act as a deterrent to conflict due to the potential for supply chain disruptions.

The article highlights the pivotal role of supply chain chokepoints, such as ASML's extreme ultraviolet lithography systems, in the production of advanced semiconductors. The reliance of global semiconductor manufacturing, including that in Taiwan, on these specific systems underscores the strategic importance of supply chain security and the potential for supply chain dependencies to influence geopolitical dynamics. Recognizing these dependencies as deterrents to conflict could fundamentally alter how nations approach international relations and conflict prevention, particularly in regions critical to global technology supply chains.

**Watch For:** How the awareness of supply chain dependencies as a deterrent influences diplomatic and military strategies, especially concerning the Taiwan Strait and US-China relations.
