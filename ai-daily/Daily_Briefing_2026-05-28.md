## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's accelerating efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem undermine US leverage through export controls.

China's development of alternatives to American chips, such as those by DeepSeek and Huawei, signifies a strategic shift towards reducing dependence on US technology. This move has second-order effects, including potential weakening of US bargaining power in trade negotiations. Furthermore, a successful domestic AI ecosystem could bolster China's position in the global tech competition.

**Watch For:** Monitoring the progress of Chinese companies like DeepSeek and Huawei in developing competitive AI chips and the impact on US-China trade dynamics.

## 2. China’s AI optimism isn’t what it seems
**Source:** ChinaTalk  
**Link:** [China’s AI optimism isn’t what it seems](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/chinas-ai-optimism-isnt-what-it-seems)  

**So What:** China's enthusiasm for AI may mask underlying fears and economic instability, influencing AI governance and policy decisions.

The interplay between optimism and fear in China's AI sector could significantly impact investment, innovation, and regulatory environments. As the Chinese population has experienced economic upheavals in the past, such as the mass layoffs of the 1990s, their perception of AI's potential might be more nuanced than it appears. This complexity could shape China's AI governance strategies and technological development pathways.

**Watch For:** Changes in Chinese government policies or investments in AI that may indicate a shift from optimism to caution or vice versa.

## 3. Control Without Ownership: How China’s Party-Business Networks Dominate Indonesia’s Mineral Supply Chains
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [Control Without Ownership: How China’s Party-Business Networks Dominate Indonesia’s Mineral Supply Chains](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/control-without-ownership-how-chinas-party-business-networks-dominate-indonesias-mineral-supply-chains/)  

**So What:** China's strategic control of critical mineral supply chains, particularly through party-business networks, enhances its position in the global semiconductor and technology sectors.

The absorption of Jiangsu Delong's Indonesian assets by Chinese firms and state-owned enterprises exemplifies China's strategy to secure access to critical minerals. This approach allows China to mitigate supply chain risks without necessarily holding direct ownership, underscoring the importance of understanding the complex networks and relationships within China's industrial base. Such control can have significant implications for the global semiconductor industry and US-China tech competition.

**Watch For:** Further examples of Chinese companies or state-owned enterprises absorbing or partnering with foreign entities to control critical mineral supply chains, especially in Southeast Asia.

## 4. The Strategic Corridor Turning Singapore Into China’s Indispensable Partner
**Source:** The Diplomat  
**Link:** [The Strategic Corridor Turning Singapore Into China’s Indispensable Partner](https://thediplomat.com/2026/05/the-strategic-corridor-turning-singapore-into-chinas-indispensable-partner/)  

**So What:** The development of the New International Land-Sea Trade Corridor cements Singapore's role as a crucial partner for China, influencing regional supply chains and tech trade.

Singapore's involvement in designing this strategic corridor indicates a deepening of economic ties between Singapore and China, potentially altering regional dynamics in Southeast Asia. This partnership could facilitate smoother trade flows for critical components and technologies, including semiconductors, bolstering China's supply chain resilience. The corridor's success could also attract other regional players, further integrating the area into China's economic sphere.

**Watch For:** Expansion of the New International Land-Sea Trade Corridor and its impact on regional trade patterns, especially in relation to technology and semiconductor supply chains.

## 5. Cisco and OpenAI redefine enterprise engineering with Codex
**Source:** OpenAI News  
**Link:** [Cisco and OpenAI redefine enterprise engineering with Codex](https://openai.com/index/cisco)  

**So What:** The partnership between Cisco and OpenAI to develop Codex reflects the accelerating trend of AI adoption in enterprise engineering, potentially shifting the landscape of AI governance and competition.

The collaboration aims to leverage Codex for scaling AI-native development, enhancing AI defense capabilities, and automating defect remediation. This could set a precedent for how large enterprises integrate AI solutions into their core operations, driving demands for more sophisticated AI governance frameworks. Furthermore, it positions OpenAI as a key player in the enterprise AI solutions market, which could influence the development of AI technologies and their applications.

**Watch For:** Adoption rates of Codex and similar AI solutions by other major enterprises, and how these technologies influence discussions around AI governance, security, and ethics.
