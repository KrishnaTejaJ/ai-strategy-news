## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's pursuit of AI independence undermines US leverage in tech competition

China's efforts to develop a domestic AI ecosystem, including alternatives to American chips, will reduce its reliance on US technology and weaken the impact of US export controls. This move is part of a broader trend of China seeking to reduce its dependence on foreign technology. As China's AI capabilities grow, the US will need to reassess its strategy for competing with China in the tech space.

**Watch For:** Monitor China's progress in developing domestic AI chips and the impact on US-China tech competition

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list

The Pentagon's decision to narrow its list of critical technologies is a positives step towards prioritization, but it may also lead to overlooked capability gaps in emerging technologies. ## 3. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** New agreements between tech firms and the US government will enhance AI model security

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate unreleased AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks will help identify potential vulnerabilities before they are exploited. This collaboration between the public and private sectors will be critical in ensuring the security and trustworthiness of AI systems. As AI becomes increasingly pervasive, the need for robust security protocols will only grow.

**Watch For:** Monitor the impact of these agreements on the development of more secure AI models and the potential for similar collaborations in the future

## 4. The Pentagon’s AI Edge Is Being Distilled Away
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The Pentagon’s AI Edge Is Being Distilled Away](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-pentagons-ai-edge-is-being-distilled-away/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's reliance on publicly released AI models undermines its AI edge

The Pentagon's use of publicly released AI models, which can be harvested by adversaries, undermines its AI edge and creates a significant security risk. As the US military becomes increasingly reliant on AI, it will need to develop more secure and proprietary AI models to maintain its advantage. This will require significant investment in AI research and development, as well as new strategies for protecting AI intellectual property.

**Watch For:** Watch for the Pentagon's efforts to develop more secure and proprietary AI models and mitigate the risks associated with publicly released models
