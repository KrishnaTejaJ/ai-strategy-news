## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's pursuit of domestic AI ecosystem undermines US leverage in tech competition

China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, including development of alternatives to American chips, accelerates its independence from US technologies and reduces the effectiveness of US export controls. This trend highlights the ongoing tech competition between the US and China, with significant implications for global AI governance. As China progresses in its domestic AI capabilities, it may further challenge US dominance in the tech sector.

**Watch For:** Monitor China's progress in developing domestic AI chips and their potential impact on US-China tech competition

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/beyond-ai-what-the-pentagon-is-missing-with-its-trimmed-critical-technologies-list/)  

**So What:** Pentagon's narrowed list of critical technologies may overlook key emerging tech gaps

The Pentagon's decision to prioritize a narrower list of critical technologies, while a positive step, may not fully address emerging technology gaps and capability shortfalls. This oversight could hinder long-term military innovation and competitiveness, particularly in areas like AI and semi-conductor supply chains. Effective prioritization and funding of emerging technologies are crucial for the US to maintain its military edge.

**Watch For:** Track the Pentagon's funding allocations for emerging technologies and their potential impact on US military innovation

## 3. The Pentagon’s AI Edge Is Being Distilled Away
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The Pentagon’s AI Edge Is Being Distilled Away](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-pentagons-ai-edge-is-being-distilled-away/)  

**So What:** Publicly available AI models undermine Pentagon's AI edge and create security risks

The Pentagon's reliance on publicly available AI models, developed by tech firms like Google and OpenAI, poses a significant security risk as adversaries can exploit these models to breach US military systems. This highlights the need for the US to develop more secure AI models and invest in domestic AI research to reduce dependence on foreign technologies. The US must reassess its AI development strategy to maintain its military edge.

**Watch For:** Monitor the Pentagon's efforts to develop more secure AI models and reduce dependence on publicly available models

## 4. Forged in a Knife Fight: China’s Brutal Domestic AI Competition
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [Forged in a Knife Fight: China’s Brutal Domestic AI Competition](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/forged-in-a-knife-fight-chinas-brutal-domestic-ai-competition/)  

**So What:** China's domestic AI competition drives innovation and undermines US AI dominance

China's intense domestic AI competition, driven by state-led innovation and strategic investment, is accelerating its AI capabilities and reducing its dependence on foreign technologies. This trend challenges US AI dominance and highlights the need for the US to reassess its AI development strategy and investment in domestic AI research. China's AI progress may have significant implications for global AI governance and the future of US-China tech competition.

**Watch For:** Track China's AI advancements and their potential impact on US-China tech competition and global AI governance

## 5. Innovation as resilience: Demand-side strategies for critical mineral supply chain security
**Source:** Atlantic Council  
**Link:** [Innovation as resilience: Demand-side strategies for critical mineral supply chain security](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/in-depth-research-reports/report/innovation-as-resilience-demand-side-strategies-for-critical-mineral-supply-chain-security/)  

**So What:** Demand-side strategies are crucial for securing critical mineral supply chains

Current policies focused on sourcing critical minerals overlook the importance of demand-side strategies, which can help reduce dependence on vulnerable supply chains. By prioritizing innovation and demand-side approaches, the US can enhance the resilience of its critical mineral supply chains and reduce the risks associated with semi-conductor production. Effective management of critical mineral supply chains is essential for maintaining US competitiveness in the tech sector.

**Watch For:** Monitor the development and implementation of demand-side strategies for critical mineral supply chain security
