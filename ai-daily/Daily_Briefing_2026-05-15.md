## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's accelerating efforts to build a domestic A.I. ecosystem undermines US leverage in the tech competition

China's development of alternatives to American chips amid US export controls signals a significant shift in the global semiconductor supply chain, potentially reducing US influence over China's tech sector. This move may also spur further innovation and investment in China's domestic A.I. industry, exacerbating the US-China tech gap. As a result, the US may need to reassess its export control strategies and consider new approaches to maintaining its technological edge

**Watch For:** Further announcements from Chinese companies like DeepSeek and Huawei on their domestic A.I. and chip development progress

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/beyond-ai-what-the-pentagon-is-missing-with-its-trimmed-critical-technologies-list/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's narrowed list of critical technologies may overlook key capability gaps and emerging threats

The Defense Department's decision to prioritize certain technologies over others may create unintended vulnerabilities, as non-prioritized areas may still be critical to maintaining a technological advantage. This could have significant implications for the US's ability to adapt to emerging threats and maintain its military innovation edge. Moreover, this decision may also impact the broader US tech industry, as Pentagon funding and priorities can influence private sector investment and research

**Watch For:** The Pentagon's upcoming budget and funding allocations to emerging technologies, as well as any revisions to its critical technologies list

## 3. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch testing of AI models by the US government marks a significant step towards AI governance and risk mitigation

This agreement demonstrates a growing recognition of the need for proactive AI governance and risk assessment, particularly in the context of national security. By evaluating AI models before launch, the US government can identify and mitigate potential cybersecurity and national security risks, potentially reducing the likelihood of AI-related accidents or malicious use. This development may also set a precedent for broader industry-government collaboration on AI governance and safety

**Watch For:** The outcome of these pre-launch tests and any resulting changes to AI model development or deployment

## 4. A U.S. Economic Security “Latency Fund”
**Source:** ChinaTalk  
**Link:** [A U.S. Economic Security “Latency Fund”](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/a-us-economic-security-latency-fund)  

**So What:** The concept of a 'latency fund' for economic security highlights the need for proactive investment in emerging technologies

The idea of a 'latency fund' suggests that the US should prioritize strategic investments in emerging technologies to maintain its economic and technological edge. This approach recognize that the development and deployment of critical technologies can take years, if not decades, and that proactive investment is necessary to stay ahead of competitors. The concept of 'nuclear latency' applied to economic security also underscores the potential risks of falling behind in key technologies

**Watch For:** Any subsequent discussions or proposals for establishing a latency fund or similar initiatives in the US

## 5. While Trump Sought Business Deals, Beijing Came Prepared to Redefine China-US Relations
**Source:** The Diplomat  
**Link:** [While Trump Sought Business Deals, Beijing Came Prepared to Redefine China-US Relations](https://thediplomat.com/2026/05/while-trump-sought-business-deals-beijing-came-prepared-to-redefine-china-us-relations/)  

**So What:** China's strategic approach to redefining its relationship with the US may have significant implications for the US-China tech competition

China's efforts to establish a 'constructive China-U.S. relationship of strategic stability' may be intended to create a more favorable environment for Chinese tech companies and reduce US leverage in the tech competition. This could involve China pushing for greater cooperation and investment in areas like A.I. and semiconductors, potentially undermining US export controls and other measures aimed at limiting Chinese tech advancement. The US will need to carefully consider its response to these overtures and ensure that any cooperation or engagement with China does not compromise its technological edge

**Watch For:** Further developments in US-China diplomatic relations, particularly with regards to tech cooperation and investment
