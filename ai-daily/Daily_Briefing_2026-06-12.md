## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's pursuit of AI independence undermines US leverage and escalates tech competition

China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, driven by companies like DeepSeek and Huawei, will reduce reliance on US technologies and weaken US export controls. This acceleration of China's AI self-sufficiency will have second-order effects on the global tech landscape, potentially altering the balance of power in the US-China tech competition. As China develops alternatives to American chips, the US may need to reassess its strategy to maintain technological leadership.

**Watch For:** Monitor China's progress in developing domestic AI chips and the impact on US export control effectiveness

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch AI model evaluation enhances US AI governance and cybersecurity

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow pre-launch evaluation of AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks mark a significant step forward in AI governance. This collaborative approach will help identify and mitigate potential risks associated with AI models, bolstering US national security and setting a precedent for responsible AI development. However, the effectiveness of this approach will depend on the scope and rigor of the evaluation process.

**Watch For:** Assess the outcomes of the pre-launch evaluations and their impact on AI model development and deployment

## 3. The Chain of Peace: Do Supply Chain Chokepoints Deter War?
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The Chain of Peace: Do Supply Chain Chokepoints Deter War?](https://warontherocks.com/the-chain-of-peace-do-supply-chain-chokepoints-deter-war/)  

**So What:** Supply chain dependencies, particularly in semiconductor production, can serve as a deterrent to conflict

The concentration of critical semiconductor manufacturing capabilities, such as those of ASML and TSMC, creates supply chain chokepoints that can be leveraged as a deterrent to war. The potential disruption of these supply chains could have devastating consequences for nations reliant on advanced semiconductors, making them a powerful tool for deterrence. This highlights the need for the US to reassess its approach to supply chain resilience and security in the context of great power competition.

**Watch For:** Monitor developments in the global semiconductor supply chain and the evolution of US policy on supply chain security

## 4. Don’t wait for a disaster to develop strong US counter-drone capabilities
**Source:** Atlantic Council  
**Link:** [Don’t wait for a disaster to develop strong US counter-drone capabilities](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/dispatches/dont-wait-for-a-disaster-to-develop-strong-us-counter-drone-capabilities/)  

**So What:** The need for robust US counter-drone capabilities is increasingly pressing, particularly in the context of great power competition

The growing threat posed by drones to national security and public safety underscores the urgency of developing effective counter-drone capabilities. While the article focuses on the World Cup, the implications are far broader, extending to potential drone-based attacks on critical infrastructure, military assets, and urban areas. The US must prioritize the development and deployment of counter-drone technologies to mitigate these risks.

**Watch For:** Track US investments in counter-drone research and development, as well as the deployment of these capabilities in various contexts

## 5. Investing in multi-agent AI safety research
**Source:** Google DeepMind News  
**Link:** [Investing in multi-agent AI safety research](https://deepmind.google/blog/investing-in-multi-agent-ai-safety-research/)  

**So What:** Advances in multi-agent AI safety research are crucial for mitigating the risks associated with complex AI systems

The funding call for multi-agent AI safety research by Google DeepMind and partners acknowledges the growing importance of ensuring the safe development and deployment of AI systems that interact with multiple agents. As AI becomes increasingly pervasive and complex, the potential risks associated with multi-agent systems will escalate, making research in this area critical for preventing accidents and ensuring public trust in AI. The outcomes of this research will have significant implications for AI governance and regulation.

**Watch For:** Monitor the progress and outcomes of the funded research projects and their potential applications in real-world AI systems
