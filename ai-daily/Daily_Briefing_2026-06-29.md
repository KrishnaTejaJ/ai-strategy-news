## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's efforts to build a domestic A.I. ecosystem undermine US leverage in the tech competition.

China's drive for A.I. independence, fueled by companies like DeepSeek and Huawei, could reduce its reliance on American chips and diminish the impact of US export controls. This move may also have second-order effects, such as accelerating the development of alternative technologies and supply chains. As a result, the US may need to reassess its strategy in the US-China tech competition.

**Watch For:** Monitoring China's progress in developing domestic A.I. capabilities and the potential for other countries to follow suit.

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch evaluation of AI models by the US government could enhance AI governance and cybersecurity.

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate unreleased AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks mark a significant step towards more effective AI governance. This collaboration may set a precedent for other tech companies and contribute to the development of more robust AI security standards. However, it also raises questions about the balance between national security and individual privacy.

**Watch For:** The outcomes of the pre-launch evaluations and potential expansions of this collaboration to other tech companies and areas of AI development.

## 3. The Defense Industrial Alliance Washington Is Throwing Away
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The Defense Industrial Alliance Washington Is Throwing Away](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-defense-industrial-alliance-washington-is-throwing-away/)  

**So What:** Deteriorating US-Canada relations may have unintended consequences for industrial security and semiconductor supply chains.

The suspension of the Canadian Permanent Joint Board on Defense and delays in the Detroit-Windsor bridge project reflect the strained relationship between the US and Canada. This deterioration may have broader implications for industrial security, potentially affecting collaboration on defense projects and disrupting supply chains, including those for semiconductors. As a result, the US may need to reassess its alliances and partnerships to maintain a stable and secure industrial base.

**Watch For:** The impact of the US-Canada tensions on defense collaborations and potential spillover effects on semiconductor supply chains and other critical industries.

## 4. China’s New Investment Regulations Block Strategic Technology Transfer
**Source:** The Diplomat  
**Link:** [China’s New Investment Regulations Block Strategic Technology Transfer](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/chinas-new-investment-regulations-block-strategic-technology-transfer/)  

**So What:** China's new investment regulations may restrict the transfer of strategic technologies, affecting global tech competition.

Decree No. 837 adds legal backing to state control over Chinese private companies, potentially limiting the transfer of strategic technologies to other countries. This move may be seen as a response to US efforts to restrict Chinese access to sensitive technologies, and it could have significant implications for the global tech landscape. As a result, other countries may need to reevaluate their investment policies and regulations to address potential security concerns.

**Watch For:** The effects of China's new regulations on international investment and technology transfer, particularly in the semiconductor and A.I. sectors.

## 5. OpenAI and Broadcom unveil LLM-optimized inference chip
**Source:** OpenAI News  
**Link:** [OpenAI and Broadcom unveil LLM-optimized inference chip](https://openai.com/index/openai-broadcom-jalapeno-inference-chip)  

**So What:** The development of custom AI chips like Jalapeño may accelerate the adoption of AI technologies and reshape the semiconductor landscape.

The introduction of Jalapeño, a custom AI chip built for LLM inference, demonstrates the growing demand for specialized AI hardware. This development may drive the adoption of AI technologies across various industries, contributing to the growth of the AI ecosystem. Additionally, the collaboration between OpenAI and Broadcom highlights the increasing importance of partnerships between tech companies and semiconductor manufacturers in driving innovation.

**Watch For:** The performance and efficiency gains achieved by Jalapeño and similar custom AI chips, as well as their potential to disrupt traditional semiconductor supply chains.
