## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's push for AI independence undermines US leverage in the tech competition and poses significant implications for global AI governance.

China's accelerated efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, driven by companies like DeepSeek and Huawei, indicate a strategic shift towards reducing reliance on American chips and technology. This move may diminish the effectiveness of US export controls and create new challenges for US policymakers. Furthermore, China's growing AI capabilities could lead to a more multipolar AI landscape, with potential implications for global AI standards and governance.

**Watch For:** Monitoring China's progress in developing domestic AI capabilities and its potential to influence global AI governance frameworks.

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch government evaluation of AI models marks a significant shift in AI governance, potentially setting a new standard for public-private cooperation in AI development.

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate unreleased AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks reflect a growing recognition of the need for more collaborative approaches to AI governance. This development may lead to more robust AI safety and security protocols, but also raises questions about the potential for government overreach and the balance between innovation and regulation. The impact on the global AI landscape and potential adoption by other countries will be crucial to watch.

**Watch For:** Assessing the effectiveness of these pre-launch evaluations in identifying and mitigating AI risks, and monitoring the potential for similar agreements to emerge in other countries.
