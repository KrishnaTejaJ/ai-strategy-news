## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's efforts to build a domestic A.I. ecosystem undermine US leverage in tech competition

China's push for A.I. independence, driven by companies like DeepSeek and Huawei, could reduce dependence on US technologies and mitigate the impact of US export controls. This development may also accelerate the decoupling of the global tech industry, with significant implications for the US-China tech competition. As China develops alternative chips, the US may need to reassess its export control strategy to maintain its competitive edge

**Watch For:** The impact of China's domestic A.I. ecosystem on the global semiconductor market and US-China trade negotiations

## 3. The U.S. Navy’s Subsea Rare Earth Vulnerability
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The U.S. Navy’s Subsea Rare Earth Vulnerability](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-u-s-navys-subsea-rare-earth-vulnerability/)  

**So What:** The US Navy's dependence on Chinese rare earths creates a significant vulnerability in its nuclear deterrent capabilities

The US Navy's reliance on rare earths refined in China for its Columbia-class ballistic missile submarines highlights the strategic risks associated with supply chain dependencies. This vulnerability underscores the need for the US to diversify its rare earth supplies, potentially through domestic production or alternative sources. The issue also underscores the interconnectedness of technological and geopolitical risks in the context of US-China competition

**Watch For:** US efforts to reduce its dependence on Chinese rare earths, including potential investments in domestic production or recycling technologies
