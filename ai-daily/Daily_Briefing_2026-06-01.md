## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's accelerated efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem undermine US leverage in tech competition.

China's push for AI independence, driven by companies like DeepSeek and Huawei, marks a significant strategic shift, potentially reducing reliance on US chip exports and diminishing the impact of US export controls. This could have second-order effects on the global semiconductor supply chain, as China seeks to reduce its dependence on foreign technology. Furthermore, this move may accelerate the decoupling of US and Chinese tech industries, exacerbating tensions in the US-China tech competition.

**Watch For:** Future announcements from Chinese tech companies on their progress in developing domestic AI capabilities and alternatives to American chips.

## 2. China’s AI Heist
**Source:** FA RSS  
**Link:** [China’s AI Heist](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/china/chinas-ai-heist)  

**So What:** China's unauthorized 'distillation' of AI models poses a significant threat to US AI governance and intellectual property.

China's alleged use of unauthorized 'distillation' to obtain sensitive AI models raises concerns about the security and integrity of US AI research and development. This could have significant implications for US AI governance, as it may require more stringent controls on AI model sharing and collaboration. Moreover, it highlights the need for more effective measures to protect AI-related intellectual property and prevent IP theft.

**Watch For:** US government responses to China's AI model 'distillation', including potential new regulations or laws to safeguard AI-related IP.

## 4. Supply chain
**Source:** Atlantic Council  
**Link:** [Supply chain](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/in-depth-research-reports/issue-brief/supply-chain/)  

**So What:** Growing risks in the AI supply chain pose a significant threat to the stability and security of AI systems.

The increasing reliance on complex global supply chains for AI components and services creates vulnerabilities that could be exploited by adversaries, compromising AI system security and integrity. This highlights the need for more robust supply chain risk management and mitigation strategies, including diversification of suppliers and development of domestic AI capabilities. Furthermore, it underscores the importance of AI governance frameworks that address supply chain risks and promote more secure and resilient AI systems.

**Watch For:** Future disruptions or attacks on AI supply chains and the development of more resilient and secure AI supply chain management practices.
