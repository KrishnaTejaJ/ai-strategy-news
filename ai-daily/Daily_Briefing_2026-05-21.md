## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's efforts to build a domestic A.I. ecosystem weaken US leverage over China's tech sector

China's acceleration of domestic A.I. development and production of alternative chips undermines the effectiveness of US export controls, potentially reducing US influence over China's tech sector. This move also highlights China's determination to achieve tech self-sufficiency, which could have significant implications for the global tech landscape. The development of domestic A.I. capabilities could also enhance China's economic and military competitiveness

**Watch For:** Monitoring the pace and success of China's domestic A.I. ecosystem development and its impact on US-China tech relations

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/beyond-ai-what-the-pentagon-is-missing-with-its-trimmed-critical-technologies-list/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's prioritization of critical technologies may overlook key capability gaps and innovation areas

The Pentagon's decision to narrow its list of critical technologies may lead to a more focused investment strategy, but it also risks overlooking emerging technologies that could address critical capability gaps. This could hinder the US military's ability to stay ahead of adversaries in key areas, such as A.I. and semiconductor technology. A more nuanced approach to prioritizing and funding emerging technologies is necessary to ensure the US maintains its military innovation edge

**Watch For:** The Pentagon's future investments in critical technologies and their impact on US military innovation and competitiveness

## 3. China’s AI Governance Offensive Threatens U.S. Tech Leadership
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [China’s AI Governance Offensive Threatens U.S. Tech Leadership](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/chinas-ai-governance-offensive-threatens-u-s-tech-leadership/)  

**So What:** China's efforts to shape global A.I. governance norms and standards threaten US tech leadership

China's diplomatic efforts to promote its A.I. governance framework and shape global norms and standards could undermine US tech leadership and influence. As a standards-setter, China could gain a strategic advantage in the global tech landscape, potentially solidifying its position as a leader in A.I. development and applications. This could have significant implications for US economic and national security interests

**Watch For:** The progress and impact of China's A.I. governance offensive, particularly in international forums such as the United Nations

## 4. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** US tech companies' agreements to test A.I. models with the government enhance national security and cybersecurity

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate unreleased A.I. models for cybersecurity and national security risks demonstrate a growing recognition of the need for collaboration between the tech industry and government. This partnership could help identify and mitigate potential risks associated with A.I. development and deployment, enhancing US national security and cybersecurity. However, it also raises questions about the balance between government oversight and industry innovation

**Watch For:** The implementation and effectiveness of these agreements in identifying and mitigating A.I.-related risks

## 5. A U.S. Economic Security “Latency Fund”
**Source:** ChinaTalk  
**Link:** [A U.S. Economic Security “Latency Fund”](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/a-us-economic-security-latency-fund)  

**So What:** A proposed 'latency fund' could enhance US economic security by promoting strategic investments in critical technologies

The concept of a 'latency fund' for economic security, inspired by the idea of 'nuclear latency', suggests a proactive approach to investing in critical technologies and promoting US economic competitiveness. Such a fund could help the US stay ahead of adversaries in key areas, such as semiconductor technology and A.I., and reduce dependence on foreign suppliers. However, the feasibility and effectiveness of such a fund would depend on its design and implementation

**Watch For:** Developments in the proposal and potential implementation of a 'latency fund' for US economic security
