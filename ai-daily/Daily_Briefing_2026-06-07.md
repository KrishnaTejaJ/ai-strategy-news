## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's advancements in domestic AI ecosystem development undermine US leverage in the US-China tech competition

China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, including the development of alternatives to American chips, accelerate its pursuit of technological self-reliance, potentially weakening US export controls as a policy tool. This move has significant implications for the global semiconductor supply chain and the US-China tech competition. As China reduces its reliance on US technologies, the US may need to reassess its approach to export controls and AI governance

**Watch For:** Monitor China's progress in developing a domestic AI chip industry and its impact on the global semiconductor market

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/beyond-ai-what-the-pentagon-is-missing-with-its-trimmed-critical-technologies-list/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's prioritization of critical technologies may overlook key capability gaps and undermine long-term military innovation

The Pentagon's decision to narrow its list of critical technologies may streamline its focus, but it also risks neglecting essential emerging technologies that could address overlooked capability gaps. This has significant implications for AI governance and the US's ability to maintain a technological edge in the US-China tech competition. The Pentagon must balance its prioritization of critical technologies with a more nuanced approach to funding and development

**Watch For:** Track the Pentagon's funding allocations for emerging technologies and assess their impact on US military innovation

## 3. The Pentagon’s AI Edge Is Being Distilled Away
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The Pentagon’s AI Edge Is Being Distilled Away](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-pentagons-ai-edge-is-being-distilled-away/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's reliance on publicly released AI models undermines its AI edge and creates a significant vulnerability

The Pentagon's use of publicly released AI models, developed by tech heavyweights like Google and OpenAI, creates a significant risk as adversaries can harvest the logic of these models, potentially breaching the Pentagon's systems. This has major implications for AI governance and the US's ability to maintain a technological edge in the US-China tech competition. The Pentagon must reassess its approach to AI development and consider more secure, proprietary models

**Watch For:** Monitor the Pentagon's efforts to develop more secure, proprietary AI models and assess their impact on US military innovation

## 4. Forged in a Knife Fight: China’s Brutal Domestic AI Competition
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [Forged in a Knife Fight: China’s Brutal Domestic AI Competition](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/forged-in-a-knife-fight-chinas-brutal-domestic-ai-competition/)  

**So What:** China's domestic AI competition is driving innovation and self-reliance, posing a significant challenge to US technological leadership

China's state-led innovation ecosystem and intense domestic AI competition are driving advancements in AI, with significant implications for the US-China tech competition. China's focus on technological self-reliance and its 'AI+' initiative to integrate AI across strategic sectors pose a major challenge to US technological leadership. The US must reassess its approach to AI governance and consider new strategies to maintain its technological edge

**Watch For:** Track China's progress in its 'AI+' initiative and assess its impact on the global AI landscape

## 5. Innovation as resilience: Demand-side strategies for critical mineral supply chain security
**Source:** Atlantic Council  
**Link:** [Innovation as resilience: Demand-side strategies for critical mineral supply chain security](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/in-depth-research-reports/report/innovation-as-resilience-demand-side-strategies-for-critical-mineral-supply-chain-security/)  

**So What:** Demand-side strategies can enhance critical mineral supply chain security and reduce reliance on vulnerable sources

Policies focused on securing critical mineral supplies have traditionally emphasized sourcing, but underutilized demand-side strategies. By adopting demand-side approaches, such as reducing waste and increasing recycling, the US can reduce its reliance on vulnerable sources and enhance supply chain resilience. This has significant implications for semiconductor supply chains and the US's ability to maintain a technological edge in the US-China tech competition

**Watch For:** Monitor the adoption of demand-side strategies in critical mineral supply chain security and assess their impact on US technological leadership
