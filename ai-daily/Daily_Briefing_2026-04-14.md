## 1. The US military needs a commercial backup plan for an AI chip crisis
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [The US military needs a commercial backup plan for an AI chip crisis](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/the-us-military-needs-a-commercial-backup-plan-for-an-ai-chip-crisis/)  

**So What:** US military readiness may be compromised by lack of commercial AI chip alternatives

The US military's reliance on specialized AI chips poses a risk to battlefield adaptability in the event of a supply chain disruption. A commercial backup plan could mitigate this risk, but would require significant investment in retraining AI models and developing compatible chip architectures. This highlights the need for diversification in AI chip supply chains to ensure national security.

**Watch For:** Announcements of US military partnerships with commercial AI chip manufacturers

## 3. China’s AI Companies Are Going Closed Source
**Source:** ChinaTalk  
**Link:** [China’s AI Companies Are Going Closed Source](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/chinas-ai-companies-are-going-closed)  

**So What:** China's shift towards closed-source AI may undermine global cooperation and accelerate the US-China tech divide

China's decision to adopt closed-source AI approaches may indicate a strategic shift towards self-reliance and reduced dependence on foreign technologies. This could exacerbate the US-China tech competition, as closed-source AI systems may be perceived as less transparent and trustworthy. The implications for global AI governance and cooperation may be significant.

**Watch For:** Reactions from the US and other countries to China's closed-source AI approach
