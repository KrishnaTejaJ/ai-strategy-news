## 3. The US is advancing AI safety through state and federal action
**Source:** OpenAI News  
**Link:** [The US is advancing AI safety through state and federal action](https://openai.com/index/advancing-ai-safety-through-state-and-federal-action)  

**So What:** A 'reverse federalism' approach to AI governance could facilitate the development of a national framework for safe and democratic AI.

The US is exploring innovative approaches to AI governance, including a 'reverse federalism' model where state laws inform national frameworks. This approach acknowledges the complexity and diversity of AI applications across different states and industries. By leveraging state-level initiatives, the US may be able to develop more effective and adaptable AI governance frameworks that balance safety, innovation, and democratic values.

**Watch For:** State-level AI governance initiatives and their potential impact on the development of a national AI framework.

## 4. When the plug gets pulled: Frontier AI, export controls, and what Africa must do
**Source:** Atlantic Council  
**Link:** [When the plug gets pulled: Frontier AI, export controls, and what Africa must do](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/africasource/when-the-plug-gets-pulled-frontier-ai-export-controls-and-what-africa-must-do/)
