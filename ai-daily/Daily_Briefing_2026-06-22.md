## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's push for AI independence undermines US leverage over China's tech sector.

China's efforts to develop a domestic AI ecosystem, including alternatives to American chips, will reduce its reliance on US technology and diminish the effectiveness of US export controls. This could have significant second-order effects, including limiting the US's ability to shape China's AI development and potentially accelerating the decoupling of the two countries' tech sectors. The development of domestic AI capabilities will also enhance China's economic and military competitiveness.

**Watch For:** Monitor China's progress in developing its domestic AI ecosystem, particularly in areas like chip development and AI model training.

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch evaluation of AI models by the US government could set a new standard for AI governance.

This move by Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate their AI models before launch could establish a precedent for greater government oversight of AI development. This could have significant implications for the development of AI governance frameworks and the balance between innovation and regulation in the AI sector. It may also create new opportunities for public-private collaboration on AI safety and security.

**Watch For:** Watch for the development of formal guidelines or regulations governing pre-launch AI model evaluation and the potential expansion of this practice to other companies and countries.

## 3. China Could Win Taiwan Without Fighting
**Source:** FA RSS  
**Link:** [China Could Win Taiwan Without Fighting](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/taiwan/china-could-win-taiwan-without-fighting)  

**So What:** China's growing economic and military influence could enable it to gain control over Taiwan without resorting to force.

China's increasing military capabilities, combined with its expanding economic influence and diplomatic efforts, could allow it to achieve its goals in Taiwan through non-kinetic means. This could have significant implications for the global semiconductor supply chain, as Taiwan is a critical hub for chip production. The US and other countries may need to reassess their strategies for supporting Taiwan and maintaining stability in the region.

**Watch For:** Monitor China's diplomatic and economic efforts to increase its influence over Taiwan, as well as the US and other countries' responses to these developments.
