## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's accelerating efforts to build a domestic A.I. ecosystem undermines US leverage in the tech competition and exacerbates semiconductor supply chain risks

China's development of alternatives to American chips, such as those by DeepSeek and Huawei, could reduce its reliance on US technology and mitigate the impact of US export controls. This would have significant second-order effects, including potentially altering the global semiconductor supply chain and shifting the balance of power in the US-China tech competition. Moreover, it highlights China's commitment to achieving AI independence, which could have far-reaching implications for global AI governance

**Watch For:** Monitoring the progress of Chinese companies in developing domestic AI capabilities and the potential response from the US government to counter China's growing tech independence

## 4. Machine Overmatch: What Salt Typhoon Reveals About China’s Data-Centric Intelligence Strategy
**Source:** War on the Rocks

## 5. What Does a ‘Constructive’ China-US Relationship Mean for India?
**Source:** The Diplomat  
**Link:** [What Does a ‘Constructive’ China-US Relationship Mean for India?](https://thediplomat.com/2026/05/what-does-a-constructive-china-us-relationship-mean-for-india/)  

**So What:** A constructive US-China relationship could have significant implications for India's diplomatic maneuvering and its position in the global tech landscape

If the US and China were to develop a more cooperative relationship, it could reduce the space for India to navigate between the two powers. This could limit India's ability to extract concessions or benefits from either side, potentially constraining its diplomatic and economic options. On the other hand, a more stable US-China relationship could create opportunities for India to engage with both powers on issues like AI governance and tech cooperation

**Watch For:** India's diplomatic outreach and engagement with the US and China, particularly on issues related to tech cooperation and AI governance, in the context of a potentially shifting US-China relationship
