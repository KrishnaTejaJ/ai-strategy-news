## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's push for AI independence undermines US leverage in the tech competition, potentially altering the global semiconductor supply chain landscape.

China's efforts to develop a domestic AI ecosystem, including alternatives to American chips, may reduce its reliance on US technology and mitigate the impact of US export controls. This could have second-order effects on the global semiconductor market, potentially accelerating the fragmentation of the supply chain. The success of Chinese companies like DeepSeek and Huawei in developing domestic AI capabilities will be crucial in determining the trajectory of this trend.

**Watch For:** Monitoring the progress of Chinese companies in developing domestic AI chips and the potential responses of the US government to this emerging challenge.

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch evaluation of AI models by the US government marks a significant shift in AI governance, potentially setting a new standard for public-private collaboration on AI risk management.

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow pre-launch evaluation of their AI models reflect a growing recognition of the need for proactive AI governance. This development may have implications for the balance between innovation and risk management in the AI sector, and could influence the development of AI governance frameworks in other countries. The effectiveness of this approach in identifying and mitigating AI risks will be critical to its success.

**Watch For:** The outcomes of the pre-launch evaluations and the potential expansion of this approach to other companies and AI applications.

## 4. Will the MATCH Act Change Chip Controls?
**Source:** ChinaTalk  
**Link:** [Will the MATCH Act Change Chip Controls?](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/will-the-match-act-change-chip-controls)  

**So What:** The MATCH Act could significantly impact the US approach to chip controls, potentially altering the dynamics of the US-China tech competition and semiconductor supply chain.

The MATCH Act, if enacted, may mark a significant shift in the US approach to chip controls, potentially leading to more stringent regulations on the export of sensitive semiconductor technologies to China. This could have significant implications for the US-China tech competition, as well as for the global semiconductor supply chain. The legislation's impact will depend on its specific provisions and the responses of other countries to the new regulatory framework.

**Watch For:** The progress of the MATCH Act through the legislative process and the potential responses of China and other countries to the proposed changes in US chip controls.

## 5. Asia Is Sprinting on AI. Europe Is Still Tying Its Laces.
**Source:** The Diplomat  
**Link:** [Asia Is Sprinting on AI. Europe Is Still Tying Its Laces.](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/asia-is-sprinting-on-ai-europe-is-still-tying-its-laces/)  

**So What:** The growing gap between Asia and Europe in AI development and deployment may have significant implications for the global AI landscape and the competitiveness of European economies.

The rapid progress of Asian countries in AI, including China, South Korea, Japan, India, and Singapore, reflects a strong commitment to investing in AI research and development. In contrast, Europe's slower pace may undermine its ability to compete in the global AI market and potentially erode its economic competitiveness. The European Union's ability to close this gap will depend on its ability to develop and implement effective AI strategies and policies.

**Watch For:** The EU's efforts to develop and implement a comprehensive AI strategy, and the potential impact of the growing AI gap between Asia and Europe on the global economy and geopolitics.
