## 1. Mutually Automated Destruction: The Escalating Global A.I. Arms Race
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Mutually Automated Destruction: The Escalating Global A.I. Arms Race](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/mutually-automated-destruction-the-escalating-global-a-i-arms-race/)  

**So What:** The escalating global AI arms race matters because it raises concerns about the potential for destabilization and unintended consequences in conflict scenarios.

The integration of AI-powered autonomous weapons into modern warfare increases the risk of miscalculation and rapid escalation, highlighting the need for governance frameworks and international cooperation to mitigate these risks. This trend is closely tied to the broader US-China tech competition, as both nations seek to leverage AI for military advantage. The development of autonomous weapons also underscores the importance of semiconductor supply chains, as access to advanced chips will be crucial for the development and deployment of these systems.

**Watch For:** Monitoring the development of international norms and agreements governing the use of autonomous weapons, such as a potential ban on lethal autonomous weapons systems.

## 2. AI & Tech Brief: Congress’s crackdown on global chip equipment
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [AI & Tech Brief: Congress’s crackdown on global chip equipment](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/ai-tech-brief-congresss-crackdown-on-global-chip-equipment/)  

**So What:** Congress's proposed legislation to expand US export controls on semiconductor manufacturing equipment matters because it reflects the intensifying US-China tech competition and efforts to restrict China's access to advanced chip-making technology.

This legislation has significant implications for the global semiconductor industry, as it could disrupt supply chains and force allied countries to choose between complying with US restrictions or risking access to the US market. The move also highlights the complexities of semiconductor supply chains, as the US seeks to balance its own economic interests with its geopolitical goals. The outcome of this legislation will have significant second-order effects on the global tech industry, particularly in terms of the distribution of market share and influence.

**Watch For:** Tracking the legislative progress of this bill and assessing the responses of key allies and industry stakeholders, particularly in countries like South Korea, Japan, and Taiwan.

## 3. Mapping the AI Governance Landscape: April 2026 Update
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Mapping the AI Governance Landscape: April 2026 Update](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/mapping-the-ai-governance-landscape-april-2026-update/)  

**So What:** The rapid growth of AI-related governance documents matters because it reflects the expanding scope of AI governance efforts and the need for a more comprehensive understanding of the AI governance landscape.

The mapping of AI governance documents to taxonomies of risks, actors, and industry sectors provides valuable insights into the evolving nature of AI governance. This research has significant implications for AI policy, as it highlights areas of convergence and divergence in governance approaches and identifies potential gaps in existing frameworks. The findings also underscore the need for more standardized and coordinated approaches to AI governance, particularly in areas like risk mitigation and compliance.

**Watch For:** Monitoring the development of new AI governance documents and assessing their alignment with existing taxonomies, as well as tracking the adoption and implementation of these documents by governments and industry stakeholders.

## 4. The US military needs a commercial backup plan for an AI chip crisis
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [The US military needs a commercial backup plan for an AI chip crisis](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/the-us-military-needs-a-commercial-backup-plan-for-an-ai-chip-crisis/)  

**So What:** The US military's need for a commercial backup plan for an AI chip crisis matters because it highlights the critical dependence of military readiness on access to advanced semiconductor technology.

The ability to rapidly retrain AI models in crisis scenarios is a key determinant of battlefield adaptability, and the US military's reliance on specialized AI chips creates significant vulnerabilities in the event of a supply chain disruption. This challenge underscores the need for diversified and resilient semiconductor supply chains, as well as the development of alternative technologies and strategies for mitigating the risks of chip shortages. The US military's pursuit of a commercial backup plan also reflects the blurring of lines between military and commercial applications of AI and semiconductor technology.

**Watch For:** Tracking the US military's efforts to develop commercial backup plans for AI chip supply chains, including partnerships with industry stakeholders and investments in alternative technologies like neuromorphic computing or graphene-based chips.

## 5. The Tech High Ground
**Source:** FA RSS  
**Link:** [The Tech High Ground](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/united-states/tech-high-ground-jake-sullivan)  

**So What:** Gaining the tech high ground over China matters because it is seen as critical to maintaining US economic and military dominance in the face of intensifying competition.

The concept of the tech high ground encompasses a range of technologies, including AI, semiconductors, and quantum computing, and reflects the recognition that technological superiority is a key determinant of national power. The US-China tech competition is driving innovation and investment in these areas, but also raises concerns about the risks of destabilization and the need for governance frameworks to mitigate these risks. The pursuit of the tech high ground also underscores the importance of semiconductor supply chains, as access to advanced chips will be crucial for the development and deployment of emerging technologies.

**Watch For:** Monitoring the US and Chinese governments' investments in emerging technologies, as well as the development of international norms and agreements governing the use of these technologies, particularly in areas like AI governance and cybersecurity.
