## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's pursuit of AI independence undermines US leverage in the tech domain

China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, including the development of alternative chips, could reduce its reliance on US technology and mitigate the impact of US export controls. This trend is significant as it reflects China's broader strategy of achieving technological self-reliance. The success of this strategy could have second-order effects on the global balance of power in the tech sector.

**Watch For:** Monitor the progress of Chinese companies like DeepSeek and Huawei in developing alternative chips and AI technologies

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/beyond-ai-what-the-pentagon-is-missing-with-its-trimmed-critical-technologies-list/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's prioritization of critical technologies may overlook key capability gaps in emerging tech areas

The Pentagon's decision to narrow its list of critical technologies is a positive step, but it must also improve its prioritization and funding of emerging technologies to address overlooked capability gaps. This is significant as it reflects the US military's need to adapt to rapidly evolving technological trends. Failure to address these gaps could have significant implications for US military innovation and competitiveness.

**Watch For:** Track the Pentagon's funding and prioritization of emerging technologies beyond AI, including investments in areas like quantum computing and biotechnology

## 3. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** US government evaluation of AI models pre-launch could enhance national security and cybersecurity

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate their AI models before launch reflect a growing recognition of the need for more proactive AI governance. This trend is significant as it highlights the evolving relationship between the US government and the tech sector on AI issues. The success of this initiative could have second-order effects on the development of more secure and trustworthy AI systems.

**Watch For:** Monitor the outcomes of the US government's evaluation of AI models and the impact on the development of more secure AI systems

## 4. The Pentagon’s AI Edge Is Being Distilled Away
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [The Pentagon’s AI Edge Is Being Distilled Away](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-pentagons-ai-edge-is-being-distilled-away/)  

**So What:** The release of cutting-edge AI models by tech companies undermines the Pentagon's AI competitiveness

The Pentagon's reliance on publicly released AI models developed by tech companies like Anthropic, Google, and OpenAI creates significant risks, as adversaries can harvest the logic of these models and undermine US military AI capabilities. This trend is significant as it reflects the challenges of maintaining a competitive edge in AI in the face of rapid technological advancements. The Pentagon must adapt its AI strategy to address these risks and maintain its military AI competitiveness.

**Watch For:** Track the Pentagon's efforts to develop and deploy more secure and proprietary AI models, and assess the impact on its AI competitiveness

## 5. Forged in a Knife Fight: China’s Brutal Domestic AI Competition
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [Forged in a Knife Fight: China’s Brutal Domestic AI Competition](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/forged-in-a-knife-fight-chinas-brutal-domestic-ai-competition/)  

**So What:** China's state-led innovation ecosystem is driving rapid advancements in AI, posing a competitive challenge to the US

China's plan to become a world leader in AI by 2030 is driving intense competition among Chinese firms, with significant investments in AI research and development. This trend is significant as it reflects China's commitment to achieving technological self-reliance and surpassing the US in AI. The success of this strategy could have second-order effects on the global balance of power in the tech sector, including the semiconductor supply chain.

**Watch For:** Monitor China's progress in achieving its AI ambitions, including the development of indigenous AI technologies and the integration of AI across strategic sectors
