## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem could reduce US leverage over China's tech sector

China's push for AI independence is a response to US export controls, which could undermine US influence over China's tech industry. This move may have second-order effects, such as accelerating the development of indigenous Chinese AI technologies and reducing reliance on US components. As a result, the US may need to reassess its strategy for competing with China in the tech sector.

**Watch For:** China's progress in developing domestic alternatives to US AI chips and the potential impact on the global semiconductor supply chain

## 2. China’s AI Governance Offensive Threatens U.S. Tech Leadership
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [China’s AI Governance Offensive Threatens U.S. Tech Leadership](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/chinas-ai-governance-offensive-threatens-u-s-tech-leadership/)  

**So What:** China's efforts to shape global AI governance frameworks could erode US tech leadership

China's diplomatic efforts to shape AI governance norms and standards could allow it to set the terms for the development and use of AI technologies, potentially undermining US leadership in the tech sector. This move is part of a broader trend of China seeking to expand its influence in international institutions and shape global rules and norms. The success of this effort could have significant implications for the future of AI development and deployment.

**Watch For:** The outcome of China's efforts to shape AI governance frameworks at the UN and other international institutions
