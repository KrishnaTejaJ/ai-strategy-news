## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's push for AI independence undermines US leverage in the tech sphere.

China's efforts to develop a domestic AI ecosystem, including alternatives to American chips, signify a strategic move to reduce dependence on US technology and mitigate the impact of US export controls. This could have second-order effects on the global semiconductor supply chain, potentially altering the dynamics of the US-China tech competition. As China accelerates its AI development, the US may need to reassess its export control policies to maintain its competitive edge.

**Watch For:** China's progress in developing its domestic AI chip industry and potential US policy responses to counter this trend.

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/beyond-ai-what-the-pentagon-is-missing-with-its-trimmed-critical-technologies-list/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's narrowed focus on critical technologies may overlook key capability gaps in emerging tech.

The Pentagon's decision to trim its list of critical technologies, while a positive step towards prioritization, may inadvertently overlook crucial emerging technologies that could address existing capability gaps. This could hinder long-term military innovation and create vulnerabilities in the US defense posture. The prioritization of funding for emerging technologies will be critical in addressing these gaps and maintaining a competitive edge.

**Watch For:** The Pentagon's future funding allocations for emerging technologies and potential adjustments to its critical technologies list.

## 3. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch AI model testing by the US government could enhance national security and cybersecurity.

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow pre-launch testing of AI models by the US government represent a significant development in AI governance. This collaboration could facilitate the identification and mitigation of potential cybersecurity and national security risks associated with AI models, ultimately contributing to a more secure AI ecosystem. The success of this initiative may serve as a model for future public-private partnerships in AI governance.

**Watch For:** The outcomes of the pre-launch testing and potential expansion of this initiative to other AI companies.
