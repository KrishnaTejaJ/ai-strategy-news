## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's pursuit of AI independence undermines US leverage in trade negotiations and tech competition.

China's accelerated efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, including alternatives to American chips, reduces its reliance on US technology and diminishes the impact of US export controls. This could have second-order effects on the global semiconductor supply chain, potentially altering market dynamics and creating new opportunities for Chinese tech companies. As a result, the US may need to reassess its strategy for competing with China in the tech sector.

**Watch For:** Further announcements from Chinese companies like DeepSeek and Huawei on their progress in developing domestic AI capabilities.

## 2. Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Beyond AI: What the Pentagon is missing with its trimmed ‘critical technologies’ list](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/beyond-ai-what-the-pentagon-is-missing-with-its-trimmed-critical-technologies-list/)  

**So What:** The Pentagon's revised list of critical technologies may overlook key areas for investment, hindering long-term military innovation.

The Defense Department's decision to narrow its list of critical technologies may prioritize short-term gains over long-term strategic advantages, potentially creating capability gaps that could be exploited by adversaries. This underscores the need for a more nuanced approach to prioritizing and funding emerging technologies, taking into account both immediate and future military requirements. The Pentagon must balance its focus on AI with investment in other critical areas.

**Watch For:** The Pentagon's upcoming budget requests and research funding allocations to see how they reflect the revised list of critical technologies.

## 3. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** Pre-launch testing of AI models by the US government could enhance national security and cybersecurity, while also informing AI governance frameworks.

This agreement between major AI developers and the US government represents a significant step towards proactive AI governance, allowing for the identification and mitigation of potential risks before AI models are released. This collaboration could serve as a model for other countries and companies, influencing the development of global AI governance standards. The outcomes of these tests will be crucial in shaping future AI policy and regulation.

**Watch For:** The results of the pre-launch testing and how they inform US government policies on AI development and deployment.

## 4. As China’s surpluses become unbearable, the EU is edging toward its own Section 301
**Source:** Atlantic Council  
**Link:** [As China’s surpluses become unbearable, the EU is edging toward its own Section 301](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/econographics/as-chinas-surpluses-become-unbearable-the-eu-is-edging-toward-its-own-section-301/)  

**So What:** The EU's potential adoption of a Section 301-like trade defense tool could significantly impact China's export-driven economy and the global trade landscape.

The EU's consideration of more assertive trade defense measures against China reflects growing concerns about the impact of Chinese industrial surpluses on global markets. If the EU were to adopt a Section 301-like tool, it could lead to increased trade tensions between the EU and China, potentially altering the dynamics of the US-China-EU trade triangle. This development could also influence China's strategic calculations regarding its trade relationships and tech ambitions.

**Watch For:** EU policy announcements and consultations on potential trade defense measures against China, and China's response to these developments.

## 5. OpenAI’s Frontier Governance Framework
**Source:** OpenAI News
