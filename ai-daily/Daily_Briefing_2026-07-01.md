## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's push for AI independence undermines US leverage in tech competition

China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, including developing alternatives to American chips, could reduce its reliance on US technology and weaken the impact of US export controls. This move is part of a broader trend of China seeking to reduce its dependence on foreign tech. The development of domestic AI capabilities could also have second-order effects, such as accelerating China's military modernization and increasing its global competitiveness.

**Watch For:** Further investments by the Chinese government in domestic AI companies and potential retaliation by the US government

## 2. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** New agreements on AI model evaluation reflect growing US focus on AI governance and risk management

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to evaluate unreleased AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks demonstrate a growing recognition of the need for AI governance and risk management. This development could set a precedent for greater collaboration between tech companies and governments on AI safety and security issues. It may also lead to the establishment of new standards and regulations for AI development and deployment.

**Watch For:** The outcome of the US government's evaluation of AI models and potential new regulations or standards for AI development

## 3. Mapping America’s Domestic Drone Supply Chain
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [Mapping America’s Domestic Drone Supply Chain](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/at-a-glance-american-quadcopter-component-manufacturing/)  

**So What:** US reliance on Chinese drone components poses national security risks and underscores need for supply chain diversification

The debate over China's dominance in drone component manufacturing highlights the risks of US reliance on foreign suppliers for critical technologies. The US government's efforts to decouple from Chinese supply chains and promote domestic manufacturing could have significant implications for the future of the US drone industry. This issue is also connected to broader trends in US-China tech competition and the need for the US to develop more resilient and secure supply chains.

**Watch For:** US government initiatives to support domestic drone component manufacturing and reduce reliance on Chinese suppliers

## 4. China’s $295 Billion Ambitions for AI Will Drive up Domestic High-Tech Stocks
**Source:** The Diplomat  
**Link:** [China’s $295 Billion Ambitions for AI Will Drive up Domestic High-Tech Stocks](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/chinas-295-billion-ambitions-for-ai-will-drive-up-domestic-high-tech-stocks/)

This investment will also reinforce China's AI capabilities, potentially widening the gap with the US in certain areas of AI research and development. The exclusion of foreign firms from China's AI data center strategy will also have implications for the global tech industry and US-China tech competition.

**Watch For:** The impact of China's AI investment on its domestic tech sector and the global AI landscape

## 5. Mapping Europe’s AI Workforce Opportunity
**Source:** OpenAI News  
**Link:** [Mapping Europe’s AI Workforce Opportunity](https://openai.com/index/mapping-ai-jobs-transition-eu)

**Watch For:** European government initiatives to address the workforce implications of AI and promote AI governance and regulation
