## 1. China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [China Seeks A.I. Independence, Weakening Trump’s Leverage](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/china-seeks-a-i-independence-weakening-trumps-leverage/)  

**So What:** China's pursuit of AI independence threatens to undermine US leverage in the tech domain

China's efforts to build a domestic AI ecosystem, including the development of alternatives to American chips, could reduce its reliance on US technology and decrease the effectiveness of US export controls. This trend is part of a broader strategy by China to achieve technological self-sufficiency. As China makes progress in this area, the US may need to reassess its approach to export controls and tech competition

**Watch For:** China's progress in developing domestic AI chip production and the potential response from the US government

## 2. China’s AI Governance Offensive Threatens U.S. Tech Leadership
**Source:** War on the Rocks  
**Link:** [China’s AI Governance Offensive Threatens U.S. Tech Leadership](https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/chinas-ai-governance-offensive-threatens-u-s-tech-leadership/)  

**So What:** China's push for AI governance leadership could erode US influence over global tech standards

China's active engagement in shaping international AI governance frameworks, including at the UN, reflects its ambition to set global tech standards and norms. If successful, this could give China significant influence over the development and application of AI technologies, potentially undermining US tech leadership. This trend is connected to a broader competition between the US and China for dominance in the tech domain

**Watch For:** The outcome of China's efforts to shape AI governance frameworks at the UN and other international organizations

## 3. Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch
**Source:** Center for Security and Emerging Technology  
**Link:** [Microsoft, Google and xAI will let the government test their AI models before launch](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/microsoft-google-and-xai-will-let-the-government-test-their-ai-models-before-launch/)  

**So What:** The US government's ability to evaluate AI models before launch could enhance national security and cybersecurity

The agreements between Microsoft, Google, and xAI to allow the US government to test their AI models for cybersecurity and national security risks before launch reflect a growing recognition of the need for collaboration between the tech industry and government on AI safety and security. This trend could lead to improved risk management and mitigation strategies for AI technologies. However, it also raises questions about the balance between security and innovation

**Watch For:** The impact of these agreements on the development and deployment of AI technologies, including any potential effects on innovation and competitiveness

## 4. China’s AI optimism isn’t what it seems

**Watch For:** Signs of growing concern or skepticism about AI in China, including any potential pushback against the technology

## 5. A U.S. Economic Security “Latency Fund”
**Source:** ChinaTalk
